Boss chronicles the life and times of Mary Emily Susannah (Minnie) Hunter, and her experiences on the frontiers of nineteenth century Australia.  Born into a family of famous horsemen and pioneers, Minnie inherits her family’s love of horses as well as an affinity with the wild landscape at the edge of settled Australia.

As a child she meets John Brewer, a self-made pastoralist and outstanding young horseman.  She quickly comes to idolise the man she calls ‘Boss’, and the friendship she strikes up marks the beginning of a devotion that lasts her whole life.

But her commitment to Boss comes at a huge cost to Minnie.  She sacrifices everything, including the prospect of marriage and a family, to support him as he struggles to build a pastoral empire from a harsh and unforgiving land. 

And to be with Boss, Minnie must also endure life with John’s passionate but wildly erratic wife, Lizzie, the woman who bears him thirteen children, but whose impulsive, restless nature is ill-suited to the lonely, isolated life of a squatter’s wife.

The book explores the frustrations and trials endured by these three, as they each struggle to realise their hopes and dreams and to find fulfilment in their lives.  Over the years their lives continue to intertwine, even after Minnie starts a new life running a boarding house in St Kilda.

Ultimately Minnie finds a kind of peace and contentment with John, but at what cost to all of them?  And has she betrayed her own moral values, when she faces ‘the worst accusation any woman could make of another’?

The book also charts the terrible fate that befell the Boandik and Wiradjuri tribes, who Minnie lived with and befriended over her life.  In the space of a generation, disease and white apathy and neglect wiped out these people and their traditional way of life.

Boss is based on the published diaries of Minnie Hunter, creatively interpreted to highlight the complex relationships between the three main characters, and to canvass their remarkable lives.
